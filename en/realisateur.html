The genius behind the camera: Robert Zemeckis

By Mélissa Poilâne

Published on 10/25/2023

Robert Zemeckis' innovative vision and direction of iconic actors that left a mark on cinema.

Back to the Future was hugely successful due to the director Robert Zemeckis, who directed the project and co-wrote the screenplay with Bob Gale. Without his involvement, the trilogy's universe might never have existed, and we might never have known this cult trilogy.
Robert Zemeckis was born in Chicago on May 14, 1952. Thanks to his cinematic career, he gained immense popularity. His first famous work is " The hunt for the green diamond " in 1984. However, it's the 'Back to the Future' trilogy that propelled his career internationally.

A Picky Director

The making of Back to the Future 2 was revolutionized by Zemeckis, who used an entirely new approach. By harmoniously merging science fiction, comedy, and adventure, he managed to create a film that defies both eras and categories. Incorporating elements of time travel and juggling complex plots with a remarkable ability to maintain narrative coherence is one of his many talents.

Zemeckis has a knack for the subtleties of cinema, possessing an admirable attention to detail. He took great care in perfecting every detail, from extraordinary sets to pioneering visuals, to ensure they were truly exceptional. The film provides a very precise glimpse of the future, with gadgets futuristic , flying vehicles, and cutting-edge technologies that appear realistic despite their fantastical nature.

a Synergy

By working with his team, including esteemed screenwriter Bob Gale and music composer Alan Silvestri, Zemeckis was able to synthesize a product that was both coherent and unforgettable. It was the collective endeavor of these creative minds that gave birth to the iconic trilogy" Back to the future ", cornerstone of the science fiction genre. Zemeckis has a talent for drawing impressive performances from his cast, which is a well-known attribute of him as a director. The roles played by Michael J. Fox et Christopher Lloyd as Marty McFly and Dr. Emmett Brown were particularly remarkable due to Zemeckis' guidance. The characters portrayed by Fox and Lloyd have become synonymous with modern pop culture, and this can largely be attributed to Zemeckis' exceptional directorial ability.

Robert Zemeckis had a significant impact on cinema by using his ability to impeccably balance humor and timing. The film Back to the Future 2 abounds with comedic and amusing moments, ensuring its appeal to a wide audience. Robert Zemeckis is a passionate cinematic artist and collaborated with an excellent team to achieve great things, thus securing his place in the history books.

Bob gale

Bob Gale is the mastermind behind the creation of the sagaBack to the future. He co-wrote the screenplay of all three films with Robert Zemeckis. Their combined efforts led to the creation of one of the most famous cinematic brands of all time. The concept of a young teenager time-traveling to meet his parents and previous life was novel at the time. Bob Gale successfully blended science fiction with humor, creating a narrative that fascinated entire generations.

Bob Gale also contributed to the creation of memorable characters such as Marty McFly and Doc Emmett Brown. Their time-traveling adventures captivated the audience and left an indelible mark in the popular culture . Bob Gale, the co-creator of this cult trilogy, has left a permanent mark on the history of cinema. His ingenuity and vision have made" Back to the future " a reference in the realm of science fiction and time-travel adventure.
